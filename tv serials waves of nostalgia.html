Golden Era Of T.V Serials

The period from 2020 to 2023 in Indian television represented a unique "Golden Era" driven by the COVID-19 pandemic, which saw a resurgence in viewership, a mix of nostalgia-driven content, and the high-stakes rise of daily soap operas centered on female empowerment and relationship drama.
Here is a breakdown of the defining trends and popular serials from 2020 to 2023:

1. The 2020 Nostalgia Wave (Pandemic Effect)


Due to the lockdown, original content production stopped, leading to the return of iconic 80s and 90s shows that broke viewership records.
Ramayan (Ramanand Sagar): Re-aired in 2020, it became the world's most-watched TV show at its peak, generating 40 million views and 650 million total views.
Mahabharat (B.R. Chopra): Returned to dominate TRP charts alongside Ramayan.

2. Top Linear TV Serials (2020–2023)


Post-lockdown, new daily soaps emerged, dominated by StarPlus and Zee TV, focusing on strong-willed female leads.
Anupamaa (StarPlus, 2020–Present): Consistently the highest TRP show, focusing on a housewife creating her own identity.
Ghum Hai Kisikey Pyaar Meiin (StarPlus, 2020–Present): A top-rated drama focusing on complex relationships and sacrifice.
Imlie (StarPlus, 2020–2024): A popular drama about a young girl from a rural background.
Kundali Bhagya (Zee TV): Remained a top-watched show throughout the pandemic period.
Taarak Mehta Ka Ooltah Chashmah (Sony SAB): Continued its streak as the most popular long-running sitcom.

3. Key Trends (2020–2023)


Female-Centric Narratives: Stories shifted from traditional, submissive bahus to strong women tackling societal issues (e.g., Anupamaa, Barrister Babu).
Rise of Regional Content: The pandemic accelerated demand for local content, with regional channels seeing high engagement.
Delayed Resumptions: While linear TV saw a huge spike in 2020 (16% increase in viewing minutes), viewership saw a 16% decline by 2022 as audiences returned to work, particularly affecting lower socio-economic segments.

4. Transition to OTT/Hybrid


While traditional TV ruled, 2020-2023 also saw the peak of Indian web series on platforms like Prime Video and SonyLIV, which are often considered the actual "Golden Era" of content quality in India.
Panchayat (2020–2023): A popular comedy-drama set in rural India.
The Family Man (2019-2021): High-stakes spy thriller.
Farzi (2023): Emerged as the most-watched Indian streaming series in early 2023.
This period marked the coexistence of traditional, sentimental family dramas (linear TV) with high-quality, edgy content (OTT), making it a diverse era for Indian television. Scam 1992: The Harshad Mehta Story (2020): Critically acclaimed, setting a new benchmark for Indian content.
